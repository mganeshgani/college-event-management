# Privacy Policy & DPDP Compliance

## Overview

This Event Management System is designed to comply with the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP), 2023** of India. We are committed to protecting user privacy and ensuring transparent data practices.

## Data Collection

### Personal Data We Collect

1. **Student Users**:
   - Name
   - Email address
   - Department
   - Roll number (optional)
   - Activity enrollment history

2. **Faculty Users**:
   - Name
   - Email address
   - Department
   - Created activities data

3. **Technical Data**:
   - IP address (for security and rate limiting)
   - Browser type and version
   - Device information
   - Access logs

### Purpose of Collection

We collect personal data for the following purposes:
- **Account Creation & Authentication**: To create and manage user accounts
- **Activity Management**: To enable activity creation and enrollment
- **Communication**: To send enrollment confirmations and important updates
- **Analytics**: To improve system performance and user experience
- **Security**: To prevent fraud and unauthorized access

## Legal Basis for Processing

Under DPDP Act 2023, we process personal data based on:
1. **Consent**: Users explicitly consent during registration
2. **Contractual Necessity**: To provide the event management service
3. **Legal Obligation**: To maintain records as required by educational institutions

## Data Minimization

We follow the principle of **data minimization**:
- Only collect data that is necessary for the service
- Do not collect sensitive personal data (religion, caste, political opinions, etc.)
- Optional fields clearly marked
- No tracking beyond essential functionality

## Data Storage & Retention

### Storage Location
- Data stored in secure MongoDB databases
- Hosted in India (or specify your region)
- Encrypted at rest and in transit

### Retention Policy
- **Active Accounts**: Data retained while account is active
- **Inactive Accounts**: Data archived after 2 years of inactivity
- **Deleted Accounts**: Data permanently deleted within 30 days of deletion request
- **Activity Records**: Retained for 2 years for audit purposes
- **Logs**: Retained for 90 days

## User Rights (DPDP Act 2023)

### Right to Access
Users can request access to their personal data by contacting privacy@eventmanagement.edu

### Right to Correction
Users can update their profile information through the dashboard

### Right to Erasure
Users can request deletion of their account and data:
1. Email: privacy@eventmanagement.edu
2. Subject: "Data Deletion Request"
3. Response time: 30 days

### Right to Data Portability
Users can export their data in CSV format through the dashboard

### Right to Withdraw Consent
Users can withdraw consent for data processing by deleting their account

## Data Sharing

We **DO NOT**:
- Sell personal data to third parties
- Share data with advertisers
- Use data for purposes other than stated

We **MAY** share data with:
- **Email Service Provider** (SendGrid): For sending notifications
- **Hosting Provider**: For infrastructure services
- **Legal Authorities**: When required by law

## Security Measures

1. **Encryption**: HTTPS (TLS 1.3) for all communications
2. **Authentication**: JWT tokens with secure hashing
3. **Password Security**: Bcrypt hashing with 12 rounds
4. **Access Control**: Role-based authorization
5. **Rate Limiting**: Protection against brute force attacks
6. **Input Validation**: Prevention of injection attacks
7. **Regular Audits**: Security assessments and penetration testing
8. **Incident Response**: 72-hour breach notification policy

## Cookies & Tracking

### Essential Cookies
- **Authentication Token**: To maintain user session (stored in localStorage)
- **Theme Preference**: To remember dark/light mode preference

### No Third-Party Tracking
- No Google Analytics
- No Facebook Pixel
- No advertising cookies

## Data Breach Protocol

In case of a data breach:
1. **Detection**: Immediate investigation upon discovery
2. **Containment**: Isolate affected systems
3. **Assessment**: Determine scope and impact
4. **Notification**: Inform affected users within 72 hours
5. **Remediation**: Fix vulnerabilities and implement preventive measures
6. **Reporting**: File report with Data Protection Authority
